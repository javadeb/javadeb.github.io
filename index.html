About Me

I hold a PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering from KU Leuven, where I also worked as a Research Assistant. My expertise lies in electromagnetics, RF and microwave engineering, wireless communications, and the integration of machine learning with RF systems.

My research focuses on wireless propagation, channel modeling, and the application of neural networks in RF and microwave systems. I have extensive experience in designing and simulating millimeter-wave channels using ray tracing techniques, as well as developing channel state information feedback systems powered by neural networks.

Currently, I work as an RF Designer, where I design high-efficiency microwave sources based on magnetron technologies. Using COMSOL and HFSS, I develop and simulate high-power electromagnetic sources in L-band and C-band frequencies.
